Frequently Asked Questions

What is the Air Quality Index (AQI)?

The Air Quality Index (AQI) is a standardized indicator developed to communicate how clean or polluted the air is, and what associated health effects might be of concern. It ranges from 0 to 500, with higher values indicating greater levels of air pollution and increased health concerns.

How many air quality monitoring stations are in Colombo?

Currently, we operate 5 monitoring stations strategically positioned across the Colombo Metropolitan Area. Each station continuously measures air pollutants and provides real-time data to our dashboard.

How can I help improve air quality in Colombo?

You can help improve air quality by reducing vehicle emissions (using public transport, carpooling), minimizing waste burning, supporting green initiatives, conserving energy, and spreading awareness about air pollution issues in your community.

Can I access historical air quality data for research?

Yes, we provide historical air quality data for research and educational purposes. Please contact us with your specific requirements, and our team will assist you with the necessary information.
